**Headline:**

The casing is 3D-printed from wood PLA — a composite of wood fibres and bioplastic. The layer lines aren't a flaw. They're what tells you it was made, not manufactured. Each one is slightly different.

## Section 4 — The Ventilation

[https://drive.google.com/file/d/1iEC0WF080kud_2gIcSsK4a0Rt6JJHgJN/view?usp=drive_web](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1iEC0WF080kud_2gIcSsK4a0Rt6JJHgJN/view?usp=drive_web)

**Headline:**
The vent on top isn't decorative.

**Copy:**
It's a voronoi funnel aperture modelled on natural cellular structures — designed to let the cold air inside without a visible fan grille. The side lattice works the same way.

It's also what makes it look the way it looks.

## What It Is

Farm by day. Defend by night.

During the day you plant, grow, and build up resources across a grid. At night, something comes for them. What you built is what you defend. A tower defence with a farming sim underneath — 2D pixel art, single player, built in Unity.

![image.png](Leaf%20Engine%20Website/image%201.png)

---

## Why This Game

It's not random that this is the launch title.

The loop — build, protect, sustain — is the same idea the console runs on. You're not just grinding for upgrades. You're thinking about what you need versus what you want.

**From the survey — things worth knowing when coding/designing:**

- "Fully repairable and upgradeable" was the #1 purchase trigger across respondents
- "A beautiful, handcrafted wooden design" was #2
- Many were worried about heat/durability → Section 3 (ventilation) addresses this implicitly
- Organic flow form won over geometric — matches the current design
- Price sweet spot in responses: €500–700 range — Base and Organic Pack tiers land here
- Print line texture: respondents split between "Premium feel" and "Unfinished" — Section 2 copy preemptively reframes it as intentional craftsmanship
